Our Story

An agency born from a belief in every child

Epiphany Foster Care was founded by Rose Katana, a social work leader with over 20 years of experience, who recognised a gap: children from Black, Asian, and minority ethnic backgrounds were consistently being placed with carers who lacked the cultural knowledge and competence to truly support their identities and wellbeing.

We exist to change that. Our agency was built on the conviction that cultural identity is not a secondary concern; it is central to every child's sense of self, belonging, and ability to thrive. We provide the specialist skills, training, and support to make culturally affirming care a reality.

While our founding expertise is in culturally responsive care, we are an inclusive agency that warmly welcomes carers and children from every background. Our trauma-informed, relationship-based, deeply child-centred approach benefits every placement we support.

Our Vision

A world where every child in care grows up with a strong sense of identity, belonging, and self-worth

We envision a care system where no child is ever made to feel different, lesser, or invisible because of their background. Where their culture, language, and heritage are not just tolerated but actively celebrated, and where the families who care for them have the knowledge, skills, and commitment to make that a reality every single day.

What We Offer

Types of fostering we provide

We offer a full range of fostering services, matching children with the right carers for their individual needs. Whether you are new to fostering or an experienced carer, we have a placement type that fits your family and skills.

Emergency Fostering

Providing a safe, immediate home for children removed from their family at short notice, often within hours. Requires flexibility and calm under pressure.

Permanent

Parent & Child Placements

Specialist Service

Supporting Unaccompanied Asylum-Seeking Children with expert, culturally informed care

Unaccompanied asylum-seeking children (UASC) are among the most vulnerable young people in the UK care system. They have often survived conflict, persecution, exploitation, and treacherous journeys, arriving alone in a country whose language, culture, and systems are entirely unfamiliar to them.

Epiphany Foster Care has developed deep specialist expertise in UASC placements. We are trusted partners of local authorities across England to provide safe, stable, and culturally responsive homes for these young people.

Specialist Cultural Matching
We work to match young people with carers who share their cultural background, language, or faith where possible, recognising how critical this is to identity and recovery.
Dedicated UASC Training
All carers supporting UASC receive specialist training in trauma, loss, asylum processes, age disputes, and culturally sensitive care.
Legal & Interpreting Support
We work with immigration solicitors and provide access to interpreting services so young people can navigate their legal journey with confidence.
Therapeutic & Wellbeing Support
Access to therapeutic consultation and specialist CAMHS pathways, recognising the complex trauma these young people carry.
Referring a UASC? We accept referrals from local authorities across England and respond to emergency UASC placements within 48 hours. Contact our dedicated UASC team directly on 0800 123 4567 or complete a referral form.

Quality & Safety

Safeguarding at the heart of everything we do

The safety and protection of every child in our care is non-negotiable. Our safeguarding framework is robust, proactive, and embedded in daily practice, not just policy documents. We operate to the highest regulatory standards and continuously review our practice.

We maintain an absolute commitment to safe recruitment, thorough assessment, and rigorous ongoing monitoring. Every carer is subject to regular reviews, and our team receives mandatory safeguarding training at least annually.

Rigorous Carer Assessment
Thorough Form F assessment, DBS checks, references, and medical clearance before any approval.
Mandatory Training
All staff and carers complete safeguarding training annually, including PREVENT, CSE, and online safety.
Quality Assurance & IRO
Independent Reviewing Officer oversight, regular audits, and outcome monitoring for every placement.
Whistle-Blowing Policy
Clear, accessible procedures for carers, children, and staff to raise concerns without fear of reprisal.

Your Journey

How to become an Epiphany foster carer

Becoming a foster carer is a significant but deeply rewarding decision. Our assessment process is thorough and supportive. We guide you every step of the way, and there are no hidden surprises.

01

Initial Enquiry

Call us, complete the online form, or request a callback. We'll have a relaxed, confidential conversation about fostering and answer your questions. No obligation, no pressure.

Same Day
02

Home Visit

A qualified social worker visits your home for an informal chat. We'll discuss your life, your family, your motivations, and whether fostering is right for you. All completely free.

Within 1 Week
03

Skills to Foster Training

Our pre-approval training programme covers everything you need to know: child development, attachment, trauma, cultural competence, and the practicalities of fostering.

3–4 Weeks
04

Assessment (Form F)

A thorough, supportive assessment of your suitability to foster. This involves DBS checks, references, medicals, and several home-based sessions with your assessing social worker.

3–4 Months
05

Panel Approval

Your assessment is presented to our independent Fostering Panel, who make a recommendation. The agency decision-maker then approves you as a foster carer.

1 Month
06

First Placement

We work with you carefully to match you with the right child. You'll receive a full placement information package, a pre-placement meeting, and intensive support in those first weeks.

Common Questions

Questions we hear most often

We know fostering can feel like a big, unknown step. Here are the questions people most often ask us, honestly answered.

Still have questions?

Our team is here to talk through any concerns, however big or small. There's no obligation and everything is completely confidential.

We warmly welcome applications from people of all backgrounds, ethnicities, faiths, relationship statuses, and family structures. You can be single, married, in a civil partnership, or cohabiting. You must be over 21, have a spare bedroom, and the emotional capacity to provide a nurturing home. While our agency has specialist expertise in culturally responsive care, we are an inclusive agency, and all carers are welcome.

No formal experience is required. What matters most is your warmth, patience, resilience, and commitment to a child's wellbeing. Our comprehensive training programme, including Skills to Foster, therapeutic parenting, and cultural competence — will give you everything you need to care confidently.

Absolutely. Many of our carers have their own children and fostering can be a wonderful experience for the whole family. We assess carefully to ensure any placement is right for both the foster child and your existing family, and your own children will receive support and preparation too.

Unaccompanied Asylum-Seeking Children (UASC) are young people who have arrived in the UK alone without a parent or guardian. They are often aged 14–17. Caring for UASC is rewarding but requires specific skills and knowledge; we provide all of this through our specialist training. You don't need to speak another language, though cultural awareness and an open mind are vital. Our UASC carers receive enhanced allowances and intensive support.

Renting is not a barrier to fostering, though your landlord's permission is needed, but many successful foster carers rent their homes. Working full-time may need to be reconsidered depending on the age of the child, but part-time work and flexible working can often be accommodated. We'll discuss your individual circumstances in our initial chat.

The assessment typically takes 4–6 months from initial enquiry to approval, including training, checks, references, and the Form F assessment. We work at a pace that suits you — we never rush assessments, because getting it right for children is more important than speed.

Extensive support: a dedicated Supervising Social Worker who visits at least every 4 weeks, monthly support group meetings, 24/7 emergency on-call, annual training, access to our therapeutic advisor, peer mentoring, and a financial support package. We don't leave you to it after approval. We walk alongside you for the entire journey.

Transferring to Epiphany is straightforward. We carry out a transfer assessment using your existing documentation, which is much faster than a full new assessment. Many carers transfer to us because they want better support, lower caseloads, or specialist expertise. We'll guide you through the process with no gaps in your placement.
