The People Behind the Care

Meet Our Team

Our team of qualified social work professionals brings lived experience and specialist expertise. We are united by one belief: every child deserves exceptional, culturally affirming care.

Chief Executive Officer Founder Responsible Individual
Rose Katana
Chief Executive Officer & Founder
MSc Social Work Leadership  ·  CQSW  ·  20+ years experience

Rose founded Epiphany Foster Care with a bold and clear vision: to transform foster care through genuine cultural competence, exceptional practice standards, and an unshakeable commitment to every child's identity and wellbeing. With over two decades of experience across statutory children's services, senior management, and agency leadership, Rose built Epiphany from the ground up as the agency she always believed the sector needed.

Under Rose's leadership, Epiphany has become one of the most respected specialist independent fostering agencies in the East Midlands, recognised for its UASC expertise, its culturally affirming approach, and the extraordinary quality of its carer support. She remains actively involved in practice, in the lives of the families we support, and in shaping policy at a national level.

"Every child who comes into care deserves not just safety — they deserve to be truly known. That is the standard I hold us to, every single day."
Dr. Amara Osei-Bonsu
Executive Director and Responsible Individual
PhD Social Work  ·  CQSW  ·  18 years experience

Amara founded Epiphany Foster Care after two decades working within statutory and voluntary sector children's services. Her doctoral research focused on identity development in Black and mixed-heritage children in care, and she is a recognised national voice on cultural competence in fostering.

"I founded Epiphany because I believe every child has the right to a home where their story is honoured, not just their placement needs."
Sarah Adeyemi-Clarke
Registered Manager
MA Social Work  ·  DipSW  ·  14 years IFA management

Sarah brings 14 years of fostering agency management experience to her role. She is responsible for the day-to-day running of the agency and ensuring that every placement meets the highest quality standards. She is passionate about the power of regulation to protect children and strengthen practice.

"Quality practice is not box-ticking. It is about real relationships and genuine commitment to children's wellbeing."
Marcus Thompson
Senior Supervising Social Worker
BA Social Work  ·  UASC Specialist  ·  11 years

Marcus leads our UASC programme and is one of the most experienced UASC practitioners in the independent fostering sector. He has worked with young people from over 30 countries of origin and sits on a national advisory group on unaccompanied minors. He trains carers and professionals across the country.

"Supporting UASC is some of the most important work in fostering. These young people show incredible resilience. Our job is to honour that."
Priya Sharma
Supervising Social Worker
MSc Social Work  ·  Therapeutic Parenting Trainer

Priya supports a caseload of long-term and therapeutic fostering carers. She is a trained therapeutic parenting facilitator and delivers our Skills to Foster and PACE training programmes. Her calm, structured approach helps carers feel confident in even the most complex placements.

"The carers I support are extraordinary people. My role is to make sure they always feel confident, informed, and truly backed up."
Dr. James Okafor
Therapeutic Advisor
DClinPsy  ·  Trauma-Informed Practitioner  ·  EMDR

James provides therapeutic consultation to our supervising social workers and direct therapeutic support to children where appropriate. He has specialist expertise in developmental trauma, complex PTSD, and culturally sensitive therapeutic approaches. He consults on our most complex and high-need placements.

"Understanding a child's trauma history is the foundation of helping them heal. I work alongside carers to make that happen every day."
Fatima Al-Hassan
Recruitment and Training Lead
CIPD  ·  NVQ Training and Development  ·  9 years

Fatima leads our carer recruitment programme and designs our training curriculum. She has a particular focus on recruiting carers from underrepresented communities and breaking down barriers that prevent people from becoming foster carers. She is a passionate advocate for inclusive recruitment.

"Our training programme is built on real experiences from carers and children. Every session is designed to make a practical difference."

How We Work

What guides our team every day

We do not just follow policy. We are driven by a shared set of values that shape how we support carers, advocate for children, and work with the professionals around each placement.

Reflective supervision for every staff member
Mandatory cultural competence training annually
Low caseloads so carers get real attention
Honest, open communication with carers and local authorities
Child's voice at the centre of every placement decision
