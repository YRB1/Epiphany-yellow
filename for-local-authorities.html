Placement Types

We cover the full spectrum of fostering need

From same-day emergency placements to long-term family homes — we match the right carer to every child, with cultural competency at the centre of every decision.

Emergency Fostering
Immediate placements at short notice, including evenings, weekends, and bank holidays. Our duty line is answered within the hour.
Response within 4 hours
Short-Term Fostering
Planned short-term placements while care proceedings are ongoing or while a permanent solution is established. Stability and continuity prioritised.
Weeks to months
Long-Term Fostering
Permanent family placements for children who cannot return home. Careful, culturally informed matching with carers committed to the long term.
Years — often to adulthood

Referral Process

Simple, fast, responsive

We have designed our referral process to be as straightforward as possible. You get a named point of contact, clear timelines, and honest communication at every stage.

1
Contact Us
Call 0800 123 4567 or email referrals@epiphanyfostercare.org.uk with the child's age, type of placement needed, and any specific requirements.
2
Initial Match
We review our approved carer pool and identify suitable matches, taking into account ethnicity, religion, language, and the child's specific needs.
3
Carer Profile
We provide a full carer profile within agreed timescales — including household details, skills, experience, and why we believe they are the right match.
4
Placement & Support
Once agreed, placement is arranged promptly. Your named SSW remains your point of contact throughout, attending all reviews and staying in close contact.
Quality Assurance

You can trust the quality of every placement

Every carer we approve goes through a rigorous assessment process. We do not compromise on quality to fill a vacancy.

Ofsted Registered
Regulated under the Fostering Services (England) Regulations 2011.
Enhanced DBS All Carers
All approved carers and adult household members hold enhanced DBS certificates.
Form F Assessed
Every carer completes a full Form F assessment before appearing before our independent fostering panel.
Ongoing Training
All carers complete a minimum of 40 training hours annually across safeguarding, cultural competence, and therapeutic parenting.
